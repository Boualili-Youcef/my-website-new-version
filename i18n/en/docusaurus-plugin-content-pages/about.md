## Profile

I am **Youcef Boualili**, a **Cloud Computing & Cybersecurity Master’s student** at the University of Lille. I am building a practical profile in **DevSecOps**, **Cloud Security** and **Kubernetes Security**, backed by hands-on projects rather than a simple list of tools.

I like working where infrastructure, security and automation meet: hardening Kubernetes workloads, analyzing cloud misconfigurations, automating checks and writing clear technical documentation.

<section id="focus">

## Current focus

My current work focuses on **security in Kubernetes**. The topics I am exploring first are:

- RBAC and privilege separation;
- NetworkPolicies and network isolation;
- admission control with Kyverno and Gatekeeper;
- policy-as-code in CI/CD pipelines;
- runtime detection with Falco;
- cloud security around AWS IAM, S3 and EC2.

My university project **k8s-sec-lab** puts this direction into practice around a
specific question: why is limited RBAC not enough to guarantee that a workload is
safe? The [technical report](/files/k8s-sec-lab-rapport-technique.pdf) documents the
scenarios, evidence and limitations of the lab.

</section>

<section id="labs">

## Labs and projects

My main projects are designed as technical proof points:

- **k8s-sec-lab**: Kubernetes DevSecOps lab with RBAC, NetworkPolicies, OPA/Conftest, Gatekeeper, Kyverno, Falco and GitHub Actions.
- **CloudSecVision**: AWS scanner focused on IAM, S3 and EC2 misconfigurations with remediation-oriented recommendations.
- **secure-card-vault-poc**: digital vault with authenticated encryption, Argon2id, access quorum and audit logging.

These projects help me practice realistic scenarios and build reusable technical foundations.

</section>
